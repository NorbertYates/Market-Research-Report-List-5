<p><p>Railway axles play a crucial role in the efficient operation of trains. There are two main types of railway axles in the market: solid axles and hollow axles. Solid axles are commonly used in heavy-duty applications as they are sturdier and provide better load-bearing capacity. On the other hand, hollow axles are lighter in weight and are preferred for high-speed trains as they reduce rotational inertia. Both solid and hollow axles cater to different needs in the railway industry, offering unique benefits based on the specific requirements of the train.</p></p>
