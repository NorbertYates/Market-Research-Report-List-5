<p><p>Animal anti-infective medicine is categorized into two main types based on their method of administration: external use and internal use. External use anti-infective medicines are applied topically to the skin or mucous membranes of animals to treat infections localized on the surface. Internal use anti-infective medicines are administered orally or injected to target infections within the animal's body. Both types of medicines play a crucial role in preventing and treating infections in animals, contributing to their overall health and well-being.</p></p>
