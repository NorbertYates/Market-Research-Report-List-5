<p><p>Electrodynamic shaker systems are divided into two main types based on their cooling methods: air-cooled and water-cooled electrodynamic shakers. Air-cooled shakers use fans to dissipate heat generated during operation, making them a more cost-effective option with lower maintenance requirements. On the other hand, water-cooled shakers use water circulation to cool down the system, allowing for higher power output and better performance in high-temperature environments. Both types are widely used in various industries for vibration testing and research applications.</p></p>
