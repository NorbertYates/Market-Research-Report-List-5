<p><p>All-season tires are commonly divided into two main market types: studded and studless. Studded tires have metal studs embedded in the tread for increased traction in icy and snowy conditions. They provide excellent grip on slippery surfaces but can be noisy and damaging to roads. On the other hand, studless tires utilize advanced rubber compounds and siping patterns to enhance traction on various road conditions without the need for metal studs. They offer a quieter and smoother ride, making them ideal for everyday driving.</p></p>
