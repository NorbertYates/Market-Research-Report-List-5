<p><p>Traditional Alkaline Electrolyzers are divided into two main types based on the electrolyte used: Potassium Hydroxide (KOH) and Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH) markets. In KOH electrolyzers, potassium hydroxide is used as the electrolyte, while in NaOH electrolyzers, sodium hydroxide is used instead. Both types of electrolyzers operate on the same principle of splitting water into hydrogen and oxygen through the process of electrolysis. The choice between KOH and NaOH electrolyzers depends on factors such as cost, efficiency, and specific application requirements.</p></p>
