<p><p>The isolating switch fuse market is divided into two main types: high voltage fuse and low voltage fuse. High voltage fuses are designed to protect electrical circuits with voltages over 1,000 volts, while low voltage fuses are used in circuits with voltages below 1,000 volts. Both types of fuses serve the same purpose of interrupting the flow of current in case of a fault or overload, protecting equipment and preventing electrical fires. The choice between high voltage and low voltage fuses depends on the specific voltage requirements of the electrical system.</p></p>
