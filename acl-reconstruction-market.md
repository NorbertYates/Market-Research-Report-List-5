Tissue Fixation involves using sutures or soft tissue devices to secure the new ACL graft in place, while Bone Fixation involves using screws and implants to attach the graft to the bone. Both methods have their own advantages and disadvantages, and the choice between the two depends on factors such as patient age, activity level, and surgeon preference. These market types cater to the diverse needs of patients undergoing ACL reconstruction surgery.</p></p>
