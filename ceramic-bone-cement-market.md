<p><p>Ceramic bone cement is a type of material used in orthopedic surgeries to fix implants or prosthesis to bone. <p><p>Ceramic bone cement is categorized into three types based on their viscosity levels - Low viscosity cements, medium viscosity cements, and high viscosity cements. Low viscosity cements are used for precise application in delicate surgeries. Medium viscosity cements are commonly used for general orthopedic procedures. High viscosity cements are ideal for load-bearing applications due to their higher strength and durability. Each type of ceramic bone cement offers unique benefits based on their viscosity levels, making them suitable for specific surgical needs.</p></p>
