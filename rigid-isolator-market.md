<p><p>Rigid isolators are used in various applications to provide a controlled environment for sensitive materials or processes. Negative pressure isolators create a vacuum to prevent contaminants from entering, making them suitable for handling hazardous substances. On the other hand, positive pressure isolators keep contaminants out by maintaining higher pressure inside. Both types of isolators are essential in industries such as pharmaceuticals, research laboratories, and biotechnology, where maintaining a sterile environment is crucial for ensuring the safety and integrity of products.</p></p>
